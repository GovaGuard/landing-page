Executive Summary

Figure 1: Sovereign Cloud Market Growth (€118.72B in 2025 → €630.93B by 2033, 23.22% CAGR)

Digital sovereignty has transitioned from a strategic consideration to a legal imperative. As we enter 2026, European organizations face a critical inflection point: the year when data sovereignty regulations shift from aspiration to mandatory compliance.

The Market Reality

The global sovereign cloud market is experiencing explosive growth, expanding from €118.72 billion in 2025 to a projected €630.93 billion by 2033—representing a compound annual growth rate of 23.22%. This growth is driven by three converging forces:

  • Regulatory Mandates: GDPR, NIS2, DORA, and emerging EU Cloud and AI Development Act
  • Geopolitical Risk: 61% of Western European CIOs are increasing reliance on local cloud providers
  • AI Sovereignty: The sovereign AI market alone is projected to reach $823 billion by 2032

1.1 What is Sovereign Cloud?

Sovereign cloud computing represents a fundamental shift in how organizations approach cloud infrastructure. Unlike traditional public cloud services, sovereign cloud is designed from the ground up to comply with specific data sovereignty requirements.

Figure 3: Three Pillars of Digital Sovereignty

The Three Pillars of Digital Sovereignty

  • Data Sovereignty: Complete control over data location, access, and processing
  • Infrastructure Sovereignty: Physical and operational control over computing resources
  • Technology Sovereignty: Independence from vendor lock-in through open standards
